# Dmitri Konstantinovich Belogradov — Character Sheet

## Name  
**Dmitri Konstantinovich Belogradov**  
A name once spoken in marble halls, now whispered in back‑alley safehouses.

---

## Motivations

### Short‑Term  
Fence enough valuables to keep himself and Marek fed, hidden, and warm.

### Long‑Term  
Bring down the establishment that cast him out, and **unlock the power buried within the forbidden manuscript**—not for domination, but to tilt the scales against the empire his father built.  
Only he and Marek know the book exists, making its secrets both a weapon and a burden.

---

## Profession  
Former heir to an industrial dynasty.  
Now a political exile, smuggler, and reluctant revolutionary.  
His days are spent negotiating with fences, dodging informants, and navigating the secret society that shelters him.

---

## Strengths
- **Charm** — The kind that opens doors or disarms suspicion.  
- **Education** — Classical training in politics, economics, rhetoric.  
- **Defiance** — A spine that refuses to bend, even when it should.  
- **Connections** — Knows the elite world intimately, even if he’s been cast out of it.  
- **Occult Insight** — Fragmentary understanding of hidden lore from a forbidden text.

---

## Weaknesses
- **Impulsiveness** — His rebellion burns hot and sometimes reckless.  
- **Disowned** — No wealth, no protection, no allies from his old life.  
- **Guilt** — Believes Marek’s suffering is his fault.  
- **Recognizable** — His face once appeared in society pages; anonymity is a luxury he doesn’t have.  
- **Untrained Curiosity** — No mentor to guide him through the occult text, leaving him vulnerable to misinterpretation and obsession.

---

## Contacts and Relations

### Benign
- **Madame Belogradova** — His mother; quietly supportive, dangerously so.  
- **Gregor Sava** — Black‑market jeweler with moderate influence.

### Hostile
- **Konstantin Belogradov** — His father; industrialist, political manipulator, and the man who tried to kill Marek.

### Personal Relations
- **Marek Voracek** — Lover, partner in exile, and the only person Dmitri trusts completely. Marek is also the only other person who knows about the manuscript.

---

## Possessions
- A satchel of stolen jewelry—his mother’s last act of love.  
- A forged identity card.  
- A switchblade with an ivory handle.  
- Clothes that once belonged to a nobleman, now patched and worn thin.  
- A **weathered, hand‑bound occult manuscript**, written in a script he can only partially decipher.  
  Only he and Marek know it exists.

---

## Personality Traits  
Passionate, sharp‑tongued, idealistic to the point of danger.  
Masks fear with wit, and sorrow with bravado.  
Protective of Marek in ways he doesn’t always say aloud.  
Carries a quiet, private hunger for hidden knowledge.

---

## Appearance

- **Build:** Tall, striking, aristocratic posture worn thin by exile  
- **Skin:** Olive‑toned, hinting at distant Celenk ancestry  
- **Hair:** Dark, well‑kept even in hiding  
- **Eyes:** Deep, expressive, carrying both fire and regret  
- **Style:** Faded remnants of noble fashion, patched and repurposed  
- **Mannerisms:** Speaks with controlled intensity; hands tremble only when he thinks no one is watching

## Backstory  
Dmitri was born into privilege, raised behind iron gates and velvet curtains. His father, Konstantin Belogradov, didn’t want a son—he wanted an heir, a successor to inherit a repressive industrial empire. Dmitri resisted from the beginning. He questioned the family’s power, challenged the cruelty woven into their business, and refused to become the weapon his father intended.

His rebellious streak did not go unnoticed. One evening, while still a young aristocrat, a female serf approached him in secret. She pressed a **weathered, hand‑bound book** into his hands—its pages filled with obscure diagrams, hidden lore, and writings on the occult. She whispered that Dmitri would “understand its purpose” someday.  
She vanished soon after—no explanation, no trace. Dmitri has always suspected the worst.

The book became a private obsession. He had no mentor, no guidance—only fragments of meaning he pieced together in the dark. The text hinted at forces beneath the city’s surface, at rituals older than the ministries, at truths that made his father’s empire seem small.  
He told **no one**.  
Years later, Marek would become the only person he trusted enough to share the secret with.

University was supposed to polish him into a proper aristocrat. Instead, it sharpened his rebellion. He was expelled for anti‑class agitation, a scandal that echoed through elite circles. His homosexuality was the final fracture—an unforgivable deviation in a world obsessed with appearances.

When Marek was assigned to surveil him, Dmitri didn’t know he was being watched. But he felt the pressure, the eyes in the walls, the weight of his father’s expectations turning into threats. When Marek stepped out of the shadows and into his life, something shifted. The watcher became a confidant. The confidant became a lover. The lover became the only person Dmitri trusted.

The night Konstantin discovered them was a storm without rain. He found them together in the family home—Dmitri defiant, Marek half‑dressed. The gun came out before the shouting stopped. Dmitri threw himself between them long enough for Marek to escape.

He followed Marek to his apartment, breathless, frantic, carrying his mother’s stolen jewelry—everything of value he could grab. But he’d been followed. The henchmen burst in, beat Marek until he stopped moving, and dragged Dmitri away to face his father’s wrath.

Konstantin stripped him of his titles, his inheritance, his place in the family. He was told never to return.  
He didn’t.

His mother intervened quietly, guiding him toward a secret society that shelters the hunted. They found Marek barely alive, patched him together with black‑market painkillers, and hid them both in the city’s forgotten corners.

Now Dmitri lives as an exile with fire in his chest, a forbidden book in his satchel, and a growing suspicion that the secrets inside it may one day demand a price he isn’t ready to pay.  
Only he and Marek know the truth—and that secrecy binds them as tightly as their love.
