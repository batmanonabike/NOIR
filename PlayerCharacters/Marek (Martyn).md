# Marek Voracek — Character Sheet

## Name  
**Marek Voracek**  
A name that sounds like it’s been stamped on too many classified files and whispered in too many interrogation rooms.

---

## Motivations

### Short‑Term  
Stay alive long enough for his bones to knit and his mind to quiet.

### Long‑Term  
Protect Dmitri from his father’s reach—and from the dark pull of the occult manuscript Dmitri studies in secret. Marek fears the book more than he fears the ministries, but he fears losing Dmitri most of all.  
He and Dmitri are the only two people alive who know the book exists.

---

## Profession  
Former surveillance technician for the Ministry of Internal Order.  
Now a fugitive living in the blind spots of Sandukar—rewiring locks, checking windows, listening to walls for microphones that may or may not be there.  
Survival is his only routine.

---

## Strengths
- **Technical Expertise** — Wiretaps, signal tracing, counter‑surveillance, encryption.  
- **Pattern Recognition** — Sees connections others miss, even when he wishes he didn’t.  
- **Endurance** — Survived beatings, torture, and the slow rot of guilt.  
- **Cold Logic** — When the panic settles, he can think like a machine.

---

## Weaknesses
- **Addiction** — Painkillers that numb the body but sharpen the shadows.  
- **Paranoia** — A survival instinct turned feral.  
- **Wanted Man** — If Dmitri’s father learns he’s alive, the hunt resumes.  
- **Trauma** — Memories of state‑sanctioned horrors that claw at him in the quiet hours.  
- **Occult Fear** — Deep unease around the manuscript Dmitri studies; terrified of what it might awaken in the man he loves.  
- **Isolation** — Only he and Dmitri know the book exists, leaving Marek alone with the burden of its implications.

---

## Contacts and Relations

### Benign
- **Dr. Oskar Velek** — Underground physician; limited influence but discreet.  
- **Sera Koval** — Former Ministry clerk who owes Marek a favor and knows how to bury a file.

### Hostile
- **Inspector Radomir Kess** — Internal Order investigator; once Marek’s superior, now convinced Marek “knows something he shouldn’t.”

### Personal Relations
- **Dmitri Konstantinovich Belogradov** — Lover, partner in exile, and the only person Marek trusts. Their bond is the one thing that survived the night everything fell apart. Marek alone shares Dmitri’s secret: the forbidden manuscript, and the danger it carries.

---

## Possessions
- A failing apartment in a forgotten district.  
- A battered motorcycle that coughs more than it runs.  
- A small lockbox containing:  
  - A stolen Ministry‑grade listening device  
  - A revolver with two bullets  
  - A bottle of painkillers  
  - A creased photograph of Dmitri  

---

## Personality Traits  
Quiet, brittle, observant.  
Speaks like every word might incriminate him.  
Cynical, but with a stubborn ember of loyalty that refuses to die.  
Haunted by the occult’s presence in their life, though he rarely admits it aloud.

---

## Appearance  
- **Age:** 50  
- **Build:** Tall, slightly hunched from old injuries  
- **Skin:** Pale, smog‑washed  
- **Hair:** Dark with streaks of grey  
- **Eyes:** Restless, scanning every corner before he steps inside  
- **Style:** Practical, layered clothing—concealment as habit  
- **Mannerisms:** Fingers twitch near pockets where tools once lived; flinches at sudden sounds

---

## Backstory  
Marek came from the grey districts—rows of tenements where the walls were thin and the nights thinner. A clever boy with a talent for wires and frequencies, he was pulled into the Ministry of Internal Order young. They trained him to listen, to watch, to record. He learned early that Sandukar didn’t need monsters; it manufactured them through paperwork and procedure.

He monitored interrogation rooms where screams were just another data point. He filed reports that sealed fates he never saw again. The job hollowed him out, one classified folder at a time.

Then came the assignment that changed everything: **surveil Dmitri Konstantinovich Belogradov**, the rebellious heir of an industrial dynasty. Marek began by watching him through lenses and microphones. He ended by watching him with something dangerously close to hope. Dmitri’s defiance stirred something Marek thought the ministries had beaten out of him years ago. He started altering reports, deleting recordings, misdirecting suspicion.

One night, the distance between watcher and watched collapsed. A kiss. A confession. A choice.

The father’s discovery was inevitable. He found them together in the family home—Marek half‑dressed, Dmitri still defiant. The gun came out before the shouting stopped. Marek escaped only because Dmitri threw himself between them long enough to break the father’s aim.

Marek fled to his apartment, bones shaking, mind racing. Dmitri arrived soon after, breathless, carrying his mother’s stolen jewelry. But he’d been followed. The father’s henchmen burst in, dragged Marek across the floor, and beat him until the world went black. They left him for dead.

He wasn’t.

Dmitri’s mother intervened quietly, guiding her son toward a secret society that shelters the hunted. They found Marek barely alive, patched him together with black‑market painkillers, and hid them both in the city’s forgotten corners.

It was there, in the dim light of their refuge, that Marek learned the truth: Dmitri carried a forbidden occult manuscript, given to him years earlier by a female serf who saw rebellion in him and trusted him with knowledge meant for moonlit hours. She vanished soon after—no explanation, no trace. Dmitri never speaks of her. Marek never forgets her.

The book radiates a quiet wrongness, a pressure in the air that makes Marek’s skin crawl. He’s spent a lifetime listening to the city’s hidden frequencies—this one feels like something listening back.

And only two people know it exists:  
**Marek and Dmitri.**

Now Marek lives as a ghost in Sandukar—an addict, a fugitive, a man who fears the occult’s shadow creeping into Dmitri’s life. He knows the father still believes he’s dead. He knows the ministries would kill for a book like the one Dmitri carries. And he knows that if the truth ever surfaces, the hunt will begin again.
